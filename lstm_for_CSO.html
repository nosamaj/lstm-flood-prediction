As this is a timeseries sequience problem we need to create a sliding window to determine what period of data we are going to take into account to make predictions.

In [33]:
# This function creates a sliding window of the dataset.
def create_dataset(dataset, sliding_window=1):
    dataX, dataY = [], []
    for i in range(len(dataset)-sliding_window-1):
        a = dataset[i:(i+sliding_window), [0,2]]
        dataX.append(a)
        dataY.append(dataset[i + sliding_window, 1])
    return np.array(dataX), np.array(dataY)

learning points¶

There are some things to note in the observed pooredicted graphs

  • The model does is not yet fit for spill count prediction as it would significasntly underpredict. I this may be a rainfall data issue, as I had the same problem using this rainfall with ICM Live
  • peak depth is reasonably predicted however there are a mixutre of over and under predictions for storms
  • The rainfall data used here is of a very course (4km grid) spatial resolution.
  • The logger and rainfall data are both of 15 minute temporation resolution. This exercise would benefit from more frequency recorded data.
  • Overall the prediction of storm reponse may well be no less uncertain thanthat of a hydraulic model verified to the above datasets
  • The model is not aware of the previous depth measurements and this is calculated purely based on rainfall and time of day. Adding other sewer monitors in the catchment could be worthwhile
  • The span of teh training data is approx 5.5 months. There are longer series available, and this model may get better with time.
  • Hyperparameters need further research on my part to optimise the model
